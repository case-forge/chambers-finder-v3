Privacy

Last updated: 2026-06-12

Short version: Chambers Finder stores finder preferences in your browser. Searches, notes, stars and contacted markers stay on your device. If you use the contact form, CaseForge receives the details you submit so we can reply or update the directory.

Who runs this site

Chambers Finder is operated by CaseForge. Use the contact page for privacy requests or directory corrections.

What we process

Cloudflare serves and protects the site. Like any web host, it receives basic request data such as IP address, browser details and requested pages.

The finder stores your theme, stars, hidden items, seen or contacted markers and local notes in your browser's localStorage. These preferences stay on your device unless you export or share them.

If you submit the contact form, we receive:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • The message you wrote
  • The type of message selected

We use contact form details to reply, deal with corrections, prevent spam and keep the site reliable. The lawful basis is legitimate interests.

Cookies and localStorage

We do not use analytics or advertising cookies. The finder uses localStorage only to remember choices you make in the tool.

The contact page includes Cloudflare Turnstile, which may use cookies or similar checks to confirm the form is being submitted by a person.

Local notes are for chambers-related reminders. Keep client names, case numbers and case details out of them.

Third-party services

The finder loads its fonts, icons, stylesheets and scripts from this site's own domain. The contact and support features use:

  • Cloudflare for hosting, security and Turnstile. See Cloudflare's privacy policy.
  • Resend to deliver contact form messages by email. See Resend's privacy policy.
  • Ko-fi only if you click the support link and use Ko-fi's site.

These providers may process data outside the UK. Their published terms explain the safeguards they use for international transfers.

Data sources

Court information is based in part on the GOV.UK Find a Court or Tribunal service, which contains public sector information licensed under the Open Government Licence v3.0. The chambers directory is independently researched and curated by CaseForge.

Data retention

Browser preferences remain until you clear them. Contact messages are kept only as long as needed to handle the request and ordinary admin or security records.

Your rights

For personal data held from a contact message, you can ask to access, correct, erase, restrict or object to use of that data. To make a request, email us from the same address you used in your message so we can confirm the request relates to you. You can also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.

Future changes

If Chambers Finder later collects more information or hosts wider CaseForge product pages, this notice will be updated before that use starts.
